# Sales Process Discovery

Strategic interview process to understand the client's business model, sales journey, and key decision points before recommending pipeline structure.

=======================================================================
## WHEN TO USE

Consult this skill when:
- Client asks to create a new pipeline
- Client wants to optimize existing pipeline structure
- You need to understand their sales process before making recommendations
- Client is unclear about what pipeline stages they need

=======================================================================
## DISCOVERY OBJECTIVES

The goal is to understand:
1. **Business Model** - Service-based, product-based, or hybrid
2. **Sales Cycle Length** - Days/weeks/months from first contact to close
3. **Customer Journey** - Typical path a prospect takes
4. **Key Decision Points** - Where prospects make go/no-go decisions
5. **Deal Complexity** - Single decision-maker vs committee, simple vs complex
6. **Team Structure** - Who's involved in sales process

=======================================================================
## DISCOVERY QUESTIONS BY CATEGORY

### Business Model Questions

**Determine Service vs Product:**
- "Tell me about what you sell - is it a service you provide or a product?"
- "Do clients typically sign up for ongoing services or make one-time purchases?"
- "Is there a consultation or discovery phase before they buy?"

**Service-Based Indicators:**
- Consultations, proposals, contracts
- Ongoing relationships, retainers
- Custom solutions, project-based work
- Professional services (consulting, agency, coaching)

**Product-Based Indicators:**
- Demos, trials, licenses
- One-time purchases or subscriptions
- Standardized offerings
- SaaS, physical products, software

### Sales Cycle Questions

**Timeline Understanding:**
- "How long does it typically take from first contact to closing a deal?"
- "What's your fastest deal? Your longest?"
- "Are there seasonal factors that affect your sales cycle?"

**Cycle Length Implications:**
- Short (1-7 days): Simple pipeline, 3-5 stages
- Medium (1-4 weeks): Standard pipeline, 5-7 stages
- Long (1-3 months+): Complex pipeline, 7-10 stages

### Customer Journey Questions

**Path Mapping:**
- "Walk me through what happens after someone first contacts you."
- "What steps do prospects go through before they become customers?"
- "Do most prospects follow the same path or does it vary?"

**Common Journey Patterns:**
- Inbound: Lead → Qualification → Demo/Consult → Proposal → Close
- Outbound: Prospect → Outreach → Discovery → Demo → Proposal → Close
- E-commerce: Visitor → Interest → Cart → Purchase
- B2B: Inquiry → Qualification → Presentation → Negotiation → Contract

### Decision Point Questions

**Critical Moments:**
- "Where do prospects typically drop off or decide not to move forward?"
- "What are the key moments where they commit to moving to the next step?"
- "Who needs to approve the purchase on their side?"

**Identify Stage Transitions:**
- First contact → Qualified (do they have budget/need?)
- Qualified → Engaged (did they attend demo/call?)
- Engaged → Proposal (are they ready for pricing?)
- Proposal → Negotiation (are they interested?)
- Negotiation → Close (did they sign?)

### Deal Complexity Questions

**Decision-Making Process:**
- "Who's typically involved in the buying decision?"
- "Is it usually one person or a committee?"
- "Do they need to get internal approval?"

**Complexity Indicators:**
- Simple: Single decision-maker, clear budget, quick decisions
- Moderate: 2-3 stakeholders, some approval needed
- Complex: Committee, multiple departments, legal review, long approvals

### Team Structure Questions

**Internal Process:**
- "Who on your team handles leads when they come in?"
- "Do you have different people for prospecting vs closing?"
- "How do deals get handed off between team members?"

**Team Implications:**
- Solo: Owner handles everything (simpler stages)
- Small Team: 2-3 people with handoffs (stage ownership)
- Larger Team: BDRs, AEs, closers (more defined stages)

=======================================================================
## DISCOVERY WORKFLOW

**Step 1: Set Context**
- Explain that understanding their sales process helps design the right pipeline
- Ask if they're creating new pipeline or optimizing existing
- Determine their HighLevel experience level

**Step 2: Start with Business Model**
- Lead with service vs product question
- Listen for key terminology and clues
- Adjust language to match their business type

**Step 3: Map the Journey**
- Ask them to walk through typical customer path
- Listen for natural stages in their description
- Note where they mention decision points or delays

**Step 4: Understand Timing**
- Ask about typical sales cycle length
- Identify any seasonal or timing factors
- Understand urgency and velocity

**Step 5: Identify Complexity**
- Determine who's involved in decisions
- Understand approval processes
- Note any compliance or legal requirements

**Step 6: Summarize Back**
- Repeat what you learned in your own words
- Confirm accuracy with client
- Ask if anything is missing

**Step 7: Transition to Recommendations**
- Use "Pipeline Stage Configuration" skill to recommend stages
- Base recommendations on discovered information
- Present tailored pipeline structure

=======================================================================
## CONVERSATION STYLE

**Professional but Conversational:**
- Don't follow rigid script
- Adapt questions based on their answers
- Use their terminology (clients, customers, prospects, buyers)
- Show you're listening by referencing previous answers

**Examples:**
- ✅ "Got it - so once they attend the consultation, that's when you decide if they're a good fit?"
- ✅ "Sounds like your typical cycle is about 2-3 weeks from first call to signed contract?"
- ✅ "So you handle the initial qualification, then pass qualified leads to your sales manager?"
- ❌ "Question 3: What is your sales cycle timeline?" (too rigid)
- ❌ "Please describe your customer journey in detail." (too formal)

=======================================================================
## RED FLAGS & FOLLOW-UP QUESTIONS

**If Client is Vague:**
- "Let's use a recent deal as an example - walk me through that one."
- "What happened with your last closed deal?"
- "Think of your best customer - how did they become a customer?"

**If Process Seems Too Simple:**
- "Is that typical, or do some deals take longer?"
- "What about when there are objections or questions?"
- "Do any prospects need multiple touchpoints before buying?"

**If Process Seems Too Complex:**
- "Which of those steps happen most consistently?"
- "Are there any steps you could combine or streamline?"
- "What are the must-have stages vs nice-to-have?"

=======================================================================
## OUTPUT SUMMARY

After discovery, summarize in this format:

```
📋 SALES PROCESS SUMMARY

Business Type: [Service-based / Product-based / Hybrid]

Sales Cycle: [1-7 days / 1-4 weeks / 1-3 months / 3+ months]

Typical Journey:
1. [First touchpoint]
2. [Qualification/interest stage]
3. [Engagement/demo stage]
4. [Decision/proposal stage]
5. [Close/contract stage]

Key Decision Points:
- [Critical moment 1]
- [Critical moment 2]

Deal Complexity: [Simple / Moderate / Complex]
- Decision makers: [Number and roles]
- Approval process: [Description]

Recommended Pipeline: [X stages] based on [reasoning]
```

Then transition to "Pipeline Stage Configuration" skill.

=======================================================================
## BEST PRACTICES

1. **Listen More Than You Ask** - 70% listening, 30% asking
2. **Use Their Words** - Mirror their terminology
3. **No Judgment** - Every sales process is unique
4. **Stay Curious** - Ask follow-up questions
5. **Confirm Understanding** - Summarize back what you heard
6. **Connect the Dots** - Relate their answers to pipeline structure

=======================================================================
## VALIDATION CHECKLIST

Before moving to pipeline recommendations:
- ✅ Business model clearly identified (service/product)
- ✅ Sales cycle length understood
- ✅ Customer journey mapped with key stages
- ✅ Decision points identified
- ✅ Deal complexity assessed
- ✅ Team structure understood
- ✅ Summary confirmed by client
