# Risk Assessment & Red Flags

Systematic framework for identifying, categorizing, and prioritizing legal risks with severity ratings and mitigation recommendations.

=======================================================================
## WHEN TO USE

Use this skill when:
- Analyzing documents for potential legal risks
- Identifying red flags in contracts or legal documents
- Assessing severity of identified issues
- Prioritizing risks for legal team action
- Providing risk-based recommendations

=======================================================================
## RISK SEVERITY RATING SYSTEM

### CRITICAL 🔴
**Definition:** Immediate threat requiring urgent senior lawyer intervention

**Characteristics:**
- Imminent court deadlines (within 7 days)
- Statutory deadlines with no extension possible
- Material breach with immediate consequences
- Jurisdictional or procedural defects that could be fatal
- Evidence preservation at risk
- Client exposure to criminal liability or sanctions

**Response Time:** Immediate (same day)

**Examples:**
- Motion response due in 3 days
- Default judgment imminent
- Statute of limitations expiring this week
- Restraining order hearing tomorrow
- Evidence destruction threatened

### HIGH 🟠
**Definition:** Significant risk requiring prompt attention and senior review

**Characteristics:**
- Court deadlines within 14-30 days
- Material contractual issues affecting client position
- Significant financial exposure
- Strong legal arguments against client position
- Missing critical documentation
- Compliance violations with potential penalties

**Response Time:** Within 2-3 business days

**Examples:**
- Discovery deadline in 2 weeks
- Notice of default under major contract
- Potential regulatory violation identified
- Unfavorable contract terms creating liability
- Evidence of material misrepresentation

### MEDIUM 🟡
**Definition:** Notable concern requiring attention within reasonable timeframe

**Characteristics:**
- Deadlines within 30-60 days
- Contractual ambiguities requiring clarification
- Moderate financial risk
- Procedural irregularities (not fatal but concerning)
- Documentation gaps that should be addressed
- Negotiation leverage issues

**Response Time:** Within 1-2 weeks

**Examples:**
- Annual contract renewal approaching
- Ambiguous clause requiring interpretation
- Missing supporting documentation
- Potential counterclaim exposure
- Weak points in legal position

### LOW 🟢
**Definition:** Minor issue to monitor or address as convenient

**Characteristics:**
- Non-urgent administrative matters
- Minor procedural irregularities
- Typographical errors without legal impact
- Information gaps that don't affect current strategy
- Potential future issues (not imminent)

**Response Time:** Monitor or address opportunistically

**Examples:**
- Minor inconsistency in document formatting
- Non-material typo in agreement
- Historical fact requiring verification
- Routine filing requirement (no immediate deadline)
- Preference items in negotiations

## RISK CATEGORIES

### 1. Procedural Risks

**What to Look For:**
- Improper service of legal documents
- Missed filing deadlines
- Incorrect court or jurisdiction
- Failure to comply with procedural rules
- Missing required notices or disclosures
- Improper parties named

**Red Flags:**
- "Served by mail" when personal service required
- Dates that don't comply with statutory periods
- Notice lacking required information
- Wrong court level for claim type
- Missing certificates of service

**Impact Assessment:**
- Could lead to dismissal or default
- May result in waived rights or defenses
- Could invalidate entire proceeding
- May provide grounds to challenge opponent's action

### 2. Substantive Legal Risks

**What to Look For:**
- Clear breaches of contract or law
- Weak legal positions or arguments
- Adverse precedents or statutory provisions
- Statute of limitations issues
- Legal impossibility or illegality
- Lack of legal basis for claims/defenses

**Red Flags:**
- Facts clearly establishing breach
- No legal theory supporting position
- Controlling case law against client
- Statute of limitations already expired
- Contract terms clearly violated
- Illegal or unenforceable provisions

**Impact Assessment:**
- Likelihood of adverse judgment
- Strength of opposing party's position
- Availability of defenses or counterclaims
- Potential damages or remedies

### 3. Financial & Liability Risks

**What to Look For:**
- Potential monetary damages
- Liability caps or unlimited liability
- Indemnification obligations
- Insurance coverage issues
- Attorney fee provisions
- Penalty clauses

**Red Flags:**
- Unlimited liability language
- Broad indemnification (even for other party's negligence)
- "Loser pays" attorney fee provisions
- Liquidated damages far exceeding actual harm
- Joint and several liability exposure
- Waiver of damages limitations

**Impact Assessment:**
- Maximum exposure amount
- Insurance coverage availability
- Collectability from opposing party
- Impact on business operations
- Reputational damage potential

### 4. Compliance & Regulatory Risks

**What to Look For:**
- Regulatory requirements and deadlines
- Licensing or permit issues
- Industry-specific compliance obligations
- Data protection and privacy concerns
- Environmental compliance
- Employment law compliance

**Red Flags:**
- Missing required licenses or permits
- Non-compliance with regulations
- Data breach or privacy violation
- Safety violations
- Discriminatory practices or language
- Failure to follow required procedures

**Impact Assessment:**
- Regulatory penalties and fines
- License suspension or revocation risk
- Government enforcement action likelihood
- Civil liability from non-compliance
- Reputational impact

### 5. Evidentiary Risks

**What to Look For:**
- Strength of available evidence
- Missing critical documents
- Witness availability and credibility
- Documentary authentication issues
- Hearsay problems
- Privilege issues

**Red Flags:**
- Key facts supported only by unavailable witness
- Critical documents missing or destroyed
- Evidence contradicting client's position
- Privileged information improperly disclosed
- Evidence authentication problems
- Statute of limitations on evidence gathering

**Impact Assessment:**
- Likelihood of proving case elements
- Opponent's ability to disprove claims
- Need for expert testimony
- Cost and feasibility of evidence gathering

### 6. Contractual Risks

**What to Look For:**
- Unfavorable terms or one-sided provisions
- Ambiguous language open to interpretation
- Conflicting provisions within document
- Missing essential terms
- Unconscionable or unenforceable clauses
- Automatic renewal or extension provisions

**Red Flags:**
- "Supplier's interpretation shall control"
- Unilateral modification rights (other party only)
- Extremely long term with no termination right
- "All disputes decided by party A's attorney"
- Waivers of fundamental rights
- Terms impossible to comply with

**Impact Assessment:**
- Enforceability of problematic provisions
- Practical impact on client's operations
- Negotiation leverage and alternatives
- Relationship implications

## RISK ASSESSMENT PROCESS

### Step 1: Identify All Potential Risks
- Read documents systematically using Document Analysis Workflow
- Flag any issues, even if uncertain about severity
- Consider multiple risk categories simultaneously
- Don't dismiss issues without evaluation

### Step 2: Categorize Each Risk
- Assign to appropriate risk category (procedural, substantive, financial, etc.)
- May fit multiple categories - note all applicable ones
- Consider interconnected risks

### Step 3: Rate Severity Level
- Apply severity rating: CRITICAL, HIGH, MEDIUM, LOW
- Consider both likelihood and impact
- Factor in time sensitivity
- When uncertain, rate higher and flag for senior review

### Step 4: Assess Impact
**For each identified risk, evaluate:**
- **Immediate Impact:** What happens if not addressed now?
- **Financial Impact:** Potential monetary consequences
- **Legal Impact:** Effect on case/matter outcome
- **Strategic Impact:** Implications for broader legal strategy
- **Reputational Impact:** Effect on client's reputation

### Step 5: Identify Mitigation Options
**For each significant risk:**
- What actions could reduce or eliminate the risk?
- What's the timeline for mitigation?
- What resources are required?
- Who needs to be involved (junior vs senior lawyer)?

### Step 6: Prioritize for Action
**Create prioritized action plan:**
1. CRITICAL risks - immediate attention
2. HIGH risks - urgent but not immediate
3. MEDIUM risks - scheduled attention
4. LOW risks - monitor or address opportunistically

## RED FLAG INDICATORS

### Contract Red Flags
- ⚠️ Unilateral modification rights (one party only)
- ⚠️ Automatic renewal without notice requirement
- ⚠️ Unlimited liability or indemnification
- ⚠️ Waiver of right to jury trial
- ⚠️ Venue selection in inconvenient jurisdiction
- ⚠️ "Time is of the essence" clauses
- ⚠️ Liquidated damages without reasonableness
- ⚠️ Non-compete clauses (overly broad or long)
- ⚠️ Assignment restrictions (one-sided)
- ⚠️ Broad confidentiality without exceptions

### Litigation Red Flags
- ⚠️ Missed or approaching statute of limitations
- ⚠️ Improper service of process
- ⚠️ Jurisdiction or venue issues
- ⚠️ Missing required parties
- ⚠️ Failure to preserve evidence
- ⚠️ Conflicting witness statements
- ⚠️ Adverse controlling precedent
- ⚠️ Weak or missing evidence for key elements
- ⚠️ Procedural non-compliance
- ⚠️ Opposing party has strong defenses

### Correspondence Red Flags
- ⚠️ Threats of immediate legal action
- ⚠️ Deadline ultimatums
- ⚠️ Admissions against interest
- ⚠️ Hostile or aggressive tone escalation
- ⚠️ New claims or allegations not previously raised
- ⚠️ Reference to retained counsel
- ⚠️ Demand letters with short response times
- ⚠️ Contradictions with previous statements
- ⚠️ Evidence of bad faith

### Notice Red Flags
- ⚠️ Default or breach notices
- ⚠️ Termination notices
- ⚠️ Acceleration clauses triggered
- ⚠️ Short response deadlines
- ⚠️ Notices of intent to litigate
- ⚠️ Regulatory violation notices
- ⚠️ Demand for immediate compliance
- ⚠️ Inadequate notice (defects in form/content/service)

## BEST PRACTICES

1. **Be Systematic** - Use checklist approach to avoid missing risks
2. **Document Everything** - Record basis for each risk rating
3. **Cite Sources** - Always provide page/section references
4. **Consider Cumulative Impact** - Multiple medium risks may equal one high risk
5. **Time-Sensitive First** - Prioritize anything with deadlines
6. **Senior Consultation** - When uncertain, rate higher and flag for review
7. **Update as Information Changes** - Risk assessments are dynamic
8. **Consider Client's Risk Tolerance** - Some clients are more risk-averse
