# Three Pillars: Visuals, Hooks, Messaging

These three pillars work together to create a magnetic brand. Each matters on its own, but combined they're unstoppable. Your vibe, your voice, your visual — that's your foundation.

---

## Pillar 1: Visuals (Stop the Scroll)

Humans process visual images 60,000x faster than text. We eat with our eyes first — this is especially true with brands. Your first impression is your visual vibe: imagery, fonts, colours, design elements.

### Visual Psychology

Use these four elements to guide emotion and attention:

- **Colour** — Trigger emotional responses strategically. Every colour carries psychological weight. Choose based on the feeling you want to create, not what looks "nice."
- **Contrast** — Grab attention and create focal points. High contrast = pattern interrupt. If everything in the feed is soft pastels, go bold. If everything is bold, go minimal.
- **Composition** — Guide the eye deliberately. Where does someone look first? Second? Control the visual journey.
- **Connection** — Make people feel something when they see your brand. Visuals should evoke emotion, not just display information.

### Anti-Branding Visual Principles

- Be unexpected, surprising, magnetic
- Stand out in a feed full of noise
- Create pattern interrupts (whatever the norm is, do something else)
- Avoid the polished, corporate "sea of sames"
- Use elements from unexpected places: zines, collage, street art, vintage ephemera, counterculture
- Mix influences — the best ideas come from pulling threads in unexpected places

### Visual Brief Questions

When creating or evaluating visual identity:
1. Does this look like everything else in the feed/industry?
2. What's unexpected about this?
3. Does this evoke a feeling or just display information?
4. Would someone stop scrolling for this?
5. Does this reflect the brand's atmosphere?

## Pillar 2: Hooks (Become a Hooker)

Your entry ticket to the attention economy circus. Every piece of content enters a fight club arena of attention. You're not just competing with your industry — you're competing with Netflix, TikTok, text messages from friends, and the pile of laundry that didn't get done this week.

### What a Hook Is

The opening line, the first few seconds, the subject line, the headline, or the first visual that stops someone in their tracks and makes them want to keep consuming your content. Without a strong hook, your message never gets heard.

### Hook Hierarchy

**Level 1: Stop the Scroll**
- Visual or headline arrest
- Pattern interrupt
- Something unexpected enough to make someone pause

**Level 2: Create Curiosity**
- Make them need to know more
- Open a loop they want closed
- Tease without giving everything away

**Level 3: Compel Action**
- Drive the click, read, watch, or buy
- Create urgency or scarcity (real, not manufactured)
- Make the next step irresistible

### Hook Principles

- **Interrupt patterns** — Whatever people expect to see, subvert it
- **Spark curiosity** — Open loops, ask provocative questions, tease
- **Call for action** — Don't just inform, drive behaviour
- **Polarise** — Make people feel something. Neutral = invisible
- **Be bold** — Boring is the biggest risk you can take

### Hook Contexts

Hooks aren't just for social media. Apply hook thinking to:
- Email subject lines (competing with 50+ other emails)
- Social media posts (competing with vacation photos, memes, news)
- Headlines and blog titles
- Opening lines of any written content
- First few seconds of video
- Website hero sections
- Ad creative
- Podcast episode titles

### Writing Better Hooks

Ask yourself:
1. Would this make ME stop scrolling?
2. Is this polarising enough? (If no one would disagree, it's too safe)
3. Does this create urgency or curiosity?
4. Is this specific or generic? (Specific always wins)
5. Could I make this more action-oriented?

## Pillar 3: Messaging (Sound Like Yourself)

Messaging is what makes people feel like they know you. Traditional branding wants you polished, professional, consistent — which makes you sound like everyone else.

### Anti-Branding Messaging

- Sound like yourself on your best day (and often your worst day)
- Write like you talk
- Curse when frustrated (if that's you)
- Get excited when talking about things you care about
- Use slang, pop culture references, conversational tone
- Your messaging should reflect who you actually are, not who you think you should be

### The Trust Chain

Authenticity → Connection → Trust → Sales

When you show up as yourself, people connect. When you're authentic, people trust you. When people trust you, they buy. Stop trying to sound professional. Start sounding like you.

### Storytelling Framework (5-Part)

Use this to craft relatable narratives:
1. **The Setup** — Where were you / your audience before?
2. **The Problem** — What went wrong? What was the struggle?
3. **The Turning Point** — What changed? What did you discover?
4. **The Action** — What did you do differently?
5. **The Result** — What happened? What's possible now?

### Messaging Principles

- **Write like you talk** — Read it aloud. If it sounds like a memo, rewrite it.
- **Be conversational** — Contractions, fragments, personality. All fine.
- **Be opinionated** — Take a stand. "I think" is powerful.
- **Be specific** — Vague = forgettable. Concrete details create connection.
- **Use signature phrases** — Develop recurring language that becomes recognisably yours.
- **Repel on purpose** — If your messaging doesn't turn some people off, it's not specific enough.

### Messaging Check

Before publishing any copy:
1. Does this sound like a person or a brand manual?
2. Would I say this out loud to a friend?
3. Is there personality in this? (Humour, frustration, excitement, vulnerability?)
4. What enemy/BS is this fighting against?
5. Is this too safe? Add some edge.
